# THE $18 BILL HEIST
### The Dumbest Counterfeit Ring in American History

**Format:** Vertical short-form (TikTok / Reels / Shorts)
**Runtime:** ~75–90 seconds
**Tone:** True-crime narration meets dark comedy
**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 (per request)
**Voice direction:** Deep, dry, slightly amused narrator — think Netflix true-crime with a smirk

## SCENE-BY-SCENE SCRIPT

### SCENE 1 — THE HOOK (0:00 – 0:06)
**VISUAL:** Extreme close-up of a freshly printed $18 bill on a wooden desk. Andrew Jackson's face, but the "$18" denomination is glaringly wrong. A magnifying glass slides into frame.
**NARRATION (V/O):**
> "In 2007, a counterfeit ring got caught… because they printed eighteen-dollar bills."

**SFX:** Cash register *cha-ching* that glitches into an error buzz.

---

### SCENE 2 — MEET THE GENIUSES (0:06 – 0:15)
**VISUAL:** Three shadowy figures hunched over an old inkjet printer in a dim garage. Stacks of paper, empty energy drinks, one guy wearing sunglasses indoors.
**NARRATION:**
> "Meet the crew. Three guys. One printer. And a plan so flawless… they forgot the United States doesn't make eighteen-dollar bills."

**SFX:** Printer whirring, paper shuffling.

---

### SCENE 3 — THE 'BUSINESS PLAN' (0:15 – 0:25)
**VISUAL:** Whiteboard with childish handwriting: "STEP 1: PRINT MONEY. STEP 2: BUY STUFF. STEP 3: RETIRE." A hand circles "STEP 2" with a red marker.
**NARRATION:**
> "Their strategy? Skip the boring fives, tens, and twenties. Go straight for the eighteen — because nobody would suspect a number that weird."

**SFX:** Marker squeak. Distant siren foreshadowing.

---

### SCENE 4 — THE FIRST 'CUSTOMER' (0:25 – 0:38)
**VISUAL:** A gas station counter at night. Fluorescent lights flickering. The clerk holds the $18 bill up. His eyebrow lifts so high it leaves frame. The customer's nervous smile starts to crack.
**NARRATION:**
> "Their first stop? A gas station. The clerk took one look, blinked twice, and asked the question that ended the entire operation:
> 'Sir… what is this?'"

**SFX:** Fluorescent buzz. Heart-thump.

---

### SCENE 5 — THE PANIC (0:38 – 0:48)
**VISUAL:** Slow-motion shot of the counterfeiter sprinting out of the gas station, dropping a fistful of fake $18s that rain through the air like sad confetti.
**NARRATION:**
> "He ran. He left a trail. A trail of evidence with his fingerprints, his face on CCTV, and an address printed on the receipt he forgot to grab."

**SFX:** Sneakers squeaking. Door chime. Wind through paper.

---

### SCENE 6 — THE BUST (0:48 – 1:00)
**VISUAL:** Federal agents in dark jackets kicking in a garage door. Inside: the printer is still running, spitting out $18 bills onto the floor. One agent picks up a bill, stares at it, and just shakes his head.
**NARRATION:**
> "Within forty-eight hours, the Secret Service was at the door. The printer was still running. Still printing. Still wrong."

**SFX:** Door splinter. Printer hum. Radio chatter.

---

### SCENE 7 — THE COURTROOM (1:00 – 1:12)
**VISUAL:** Three defendants sit in a wood-paneled courtroom. The judge holds up a $18 bill, expression deadpan. The bailiff is biting his lip to keep from laughing.
**NARRATION:**
> "In court, the judge held up the evidence and asked the only question that mattered:
> 'Did none of you… check?'"

**SFX:** Gavel tap. Quiet courtroom murmur.

---

### SCENE 8 — THE PUNCHLINE (1:12 – 1:22)
**VISUAL:** A framed $18 bill hanging on a wall at the Secret Service training academy. A plaque underneath reads: "WHAT NOT TO DO."
**NARRATION:**
> "Today, that bill hangs in a training museum — a permanent reminder that crime doesn't pay…
> especially when your denomination doesn't exist."

**SFX:** Soft museum ambience. Single piano note.

---

### SCENE 9 — CTA / OUTRO (1:22 – 1:30)
**VISUAL:** Text overlay: "FOLLOW FOR MORE OF THE DUMBEST CRIMES EVER COMMITTED." Bill slowly burns at the corner.
**NARRATION:**
> "Follow — because somewhere out there… someone's printing twenty-three-dollar bills right now."

**SFX:** Match strike. Fire crackle. Hard cut to black.
